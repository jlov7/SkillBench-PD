Non-technical briefing

The core problem: context bloat without proof.

Teams often add more instructions to prompts assuming quality will improve. In practice, this can increase latency and cost and sometimes hurts consistency. SkillBench-PD exists to replace assumptions with measurable evidence.

What was built

An experiment framework that compares prompt strategies on controlled tasks and outputs shareable reports.

Why it matters

It helps teams choose safer, cheaper, and faster prompting approaches with statistical confidence.

What “good” looks like

Progressive disclosure should hold quality while reducing unnecessary tokens and keeping latency predictable.

How to pitch this in 30 seconds

“This benchmark lets us quantify the impact of prompt loading strategies before shipping. We can prove tradeoffs across cost, speed, and quality, then enforce regression gates in CI so we do not drift.”
